About

My interests sit at the intersection of machine learning research and real-world systems. I focus on building models that are not only powerful, but also reliable, efficient, and usable in real environments. Much of my work involves taking ideas from research and turning them into systems that can actually run in production.

I've worked on problems across deep learning, representation learning, and applied AI. At Wadhwani AI, I helped build a diabetic retinopathy screening system with clinicians at AIIMS Delhi, designing models and data pipelines for the constraints of real healthcare deployments in India.

At UW–Madison, my research focuses on understanding the learning dynamics of deep networks, specifically how depth, width, and structure shape what models can learn. I'm also interested in bias mitigation, mechanistic interpretability, and generalization from limited data.

I enjoy working at the boundary between research, engineering, and impact. The most exciting problems to me are the ones that require both careful thinking and building real systems.

A Mechanistic Interpretability Study of Code vs Math Reasoning in LLMs

Are math and code reasoning the same skill inside a language model? We studied the Qwen 2.5 model family using SVD on internal activations to identify which weights drive each capability. The surprising finding: the correlation between the two tasks is asymmetric. Code reasoning shares structure with math, but not the other way around.
